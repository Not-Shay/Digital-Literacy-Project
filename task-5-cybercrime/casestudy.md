## Case Study: UPI Fraud

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and individuals who frequently use digital payment applications. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by making them approve fraudulent payment requests.

A common method used by fraudsters is sending fake payment links or QR codes. The victim is told that they will receive money, but instead, they are asked to enter their UPI PIN, which results in money being debited from their account. In some cases, attackers impersonate bank officials or customer care representatives to gain trust.

Students and inexperienced users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, stress, and sometimes difficulty in recovering the lost money.

This case study highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment systems.
